**The Short Answer:** Your teams play in up to three types of competitions — their domestic league, their country's cup, and European tournaments. All matches count. Different competitions award different points.

---

## Why This Matters

Fantasy Fútbol is the only fantasy soccer game that counts **every match** your teams play.

Traditional fantasy games only track league matches. We track everything:

- Saturday league game? **Counts.**
- Midweek Champions League night? **Counts.**
- FA Cup third round against a lower-league side? **Counts.**

A team competing on multiple fronts has more chances to score points — but also more chances to slip up.

---

## The Three Competition Types

| Competition Type | Examples | Win Points | Draw Points |
|------------------|----------|------------|-------------|
| Domestic League | Premier League, La Liga, Serie A | +3 | +1 |
| Domestic Cup | FA Cup, Copa del Rey, DFB-Pokal | +4 | +2 |
| European Tournament | Champions League, Europa League | +5 | +2 |

*Goal differential (+1 per goal scored, -1 per goal conceded) applies to all competition types equally.*

## 1. Domestic Leagues

### What They Are

The bread and butter of European football. Every team plays every other team twice — once home, once away — over the course of the season.

### The Big 5 Leagues

| League | Country | Teams | Matches Per Team |
|--------|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Premier League | England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 | 20 | 38 |
| La Liga | Spain 🇪🇸 | 20 | 38 |
| Bundesliga | Germany 🇩🇪 | 18 | 34 |
| Serie A | Italy 🇮🇹 | 20 | 38 |
| Ligue 1 | France 🇫🇷 | 20 | 38 |

### Points

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win | +3 |
| Draw | +1 |
| Loss | 0 |

### Strategic Notes

- **Every team plays in a league.** This is the baseline — all 98 draftable teams have league matches.
- **Fixture difficulty varies wildly.** December might have your team facing three top-6 sides; January might be easy opponents.
- **The differentiator is what else they're competing in.**

## 2. Domestic Cups

### What They Are

Knockout tournaments within each country. Lose and you're out. Win and you advance.

### The Big 5 Cups

| Cup | Country |
|-----|---------|
| FA Cup | England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 |
| EFL Cup (Carabao Cup) | England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 |
| Copa del Rey | Spain 🇪🇸 |
| DFB-Pokal | Germany 🇩🇪 |
| Coppa Italia | Italy 🇮🇹 |
| Coupe de France | France 🇫🇷 |

> **Note:** England has two domestic cups (FA Cup and EFL Cup). The other countries have one primary cup competition.

### Schedule

- **Early rounds:** September – January
- **Quarterfinals:** January – February
- **Semifinals:** February – April
- **Final:** May

### Points

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win | +4 |
| Draw | +2 |
| Loss | 0 |

### Strategic Notes

- **Knockout = volatility.** Your team might play 6 cup matches (final run) or just 1 (early exit).
- **Cup draws matter.** Getting drawn against a lower-league side is a gift. A top-6 opponent in the third round is a nightmare.
- **England is cup-heavy.** Teams in both the FA Cup and EFL Cup have more opportunities.

## 3. European Tournaments

### What They Are

The pinnacle of club football. Teams from across Europe compete in continental competitions organized by UEFA.

### The Three European Competitions

| Tournament | Prestige | Qualification |
|------------|----------|---------------|
| UEFA Champions League | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Top finishers from each league |
| UEFA Europa League | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Next tier + cup winners + CL dropouts |
| UEFA Conference League | ⭐⭐⭐ | Lower qualifiers + EL dropouts |

### Current Format (2024-25 onwards)

**League Phase (September – January):**
- 36 teams in each competition
- Each team plays 8 matches against 8 different opponents
- Top 8 advance directly to Round of 16; teams 9-24 play a playoff round; teams 25-36 eliminated

**Knockout Phase (February – May):**
- Single-elimination bracket (two-legged ties until the final)
- Final is a single match at a neutral venue

### Schedule

| Phase | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| League Phase (Matchdays 1-8) | September – January |
| Knockout Playoffs | February |
| Round of 16 | February – March |
| Quarterfinals | April |
| Semifinals | April – May |
| Final | Late May / Early June |

### Points

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win | +5 |
| Draw | +2 |
| Loss | 0 |

### Strategic Notes

- **European teams are premium assets.** Those +5 win bonuses add up fast.
- **Deep runs are goldmines.** A team reaching the Champions League final plays 15+ European matches.
- **Know who's in what.** Before your draft, know which teams qualified for which European competition. This is critical information.

## The European Advantage (With Math)

### Team A: Brighton (No Europe) — hypothetical December

- 6 Premier League + 1 FA Cup
- If they win everything: 6×+3 + 1×+4 = **+22 match result points**

### Team B: Arsenal (Champions League) — same December

- 5 Premier League + 2 Champions League + 1 FA Cup
- If they win everything: 5×+3 + 2×+5 + 1×+4 = **+29 match result points**

Even with one fewer league match, Arsenal's European football is worth **+7 more points** in pure win bonuses. *This is why European teams are drafted early.*

## Extra Time and Penalties

Cup matches (domestic and European knockouts) can go beyond 90 minutes.

**Goal Differential:**
- Goals in extra time **count**
- Penalty shootout goals **do not count**

**Match Result:**
- The team that advances gets the **win bonus**
- The team eliminated gets the **loss** (0 points)

**Example: FA Cup match goes to penalties**

Manchester United 2-2 Liverpool (after ET). Liverpool wins 5-4 on penalties.

- Liverpool GD: +2 scored, -2 conceded = **0**
- Liverpool MRP: Cup win = **+4**
- **Liverpool total: +4**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do friendlies or preseason matches count?

No. Only official competitive matches: domestic leagues, domestic cups, and UEFA European tournaments.

### Do two-legged ties count as two matches?

Yes. Each leg is scored independently.

### Is Conference League worth targeting?

Yes, but with caveats. Conference League teams get the same +5 for a win as Champions League teams, but the teams in Conference League are generally weaker domestically. Still valuable compared to no European football.

### What if a team drops from Champions League to Europa League?

The new format (2024-25 onwards) eliminates dropping down. Teams finishing 25th-36th in the CL league phase are eliminated entirely.
