**The Short Answer:** You draft 10 teams, but only 5 earn you points each month. Before each month begins, you choose which 5 teams start and which 5 sit on the bench.

---

## How It Works

When you draft, you select **10 teams** for your roster.

Each month, you divide them:

| Role | Teams | Earns Points? |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Starting 5** | 5 teams | ✅ Yes |
| **Bench** | 5 teams | ❌ No |

Your Starting 5 earn you points from every match they play that month — league games, cup matches, European nights, all of it.

Your Bench teams still play their real-world matches, but those results don't count toward your Fantasy Fútbol score.

---

## Why This System Exists

This is where strategy lives in Fantasy Fútbol.

Not every month is created equal for every team:

- **Fixture congestion:** Some teams have 8 matches in a month, others have 4
- **Competition schedule:** Champions League group stages hit certain months hard
- **Opponent difficulty:** December might be brutal for one team, easy for another
- **Cup draws:** Your team might face Manchester City in the cup, or a League Two side

The Starting 5 system forces you to think ahead. Which of your teams are set up for a big month?

---

## The Monthly Lock

**Your Starting 5 locks at the beginning of each month.**

Once the month's first matches begin, your lineup is frozen. You cannot swap a starter for a bench team mid-month.

> **Important:** You'll receive a notification reminder before each lock.

---

## Making Your Monthly Decision

Before each month locks, ask these questions about each team on your roster:

### 1. How many matches do they have?

More matches = more chances to score points (and more chances to lose them). Check the fixture list. A team with 7 matches has nearly double the scoring potential of a team with 4.

### 2. What competitions are they in?

European matches are worth more:

| Competition | Win Points |
|-------------|------------|
| Domestic League | +3 |
| Domestic Cup | +4 |
| European | +5 |

A team playing two Champions League matches might outscore a team with three league games.

### 3. Who are they playing?

Opponent strength matters enormously. Your team facing Nottingham Forest, Burnley, and Sheffield United has a very different outlook than one facing Arsenal, Liverpool, and Manchester City.

### 4. What's their current form?

Is the team on a hot streak or struggling? Are key players injured? Did they just sack their manager? Real-world context should influence your decisions.

### 5. What about your bench teams?

This is relative. You're not asking "is Real Madrid good?" — you're asking "is Real Madrid's November better than Athletic Bilbao's November?" Compare your options before you commit.

---

## What Happens to Bench Teams?

Your bench teams play their matches, but you don't earn or lose points from them.

**In the app, you can still see:**
- Their match results
- Their goal differential
- Their match result points

But those points appear faded/greyed out and don't add to your total.

**Why show them at all?** So you can track what you're missing. If your bench team has a monster month, that's useful information for next month's decision.

---

## Can I Change My Lineup Mid-Month?

**No.** Once the month locks, your Starting 5 is fixed.

This is intentional. It rewards managers who plan ahead and punishes reactive decision-making.

---

## Strategic Concepts

### Fixture Stacking

Some months have two Champions League matchdays. The months with heavy European schedules are goldmines for teams in that competition. A team with 2 European matches in a month can earn +10 points just from wins — before goal differential.

### The Cup Wildcard

Cup competitions are knockout. A deep cup run can be incredibly valuable, but it's hard to predict. Consider which of your teams are still alive in which cups.

### The Congestion Trap

More matches isn't always better. A team playing 8 matches in 30 days might rotate heavily, play tired, and actually underperform a rested team playing 5 matches. Quality vs. quantity is a real consideration.

### Benchable Stars

Sometimes your best team on paper should be benched. If Barcelona has 4 matches against top-6 opponents while Real Sociedad has 5 matches against relegation candidates, the "worse" team might be the better start.

Don't fall in love with names. Follow the fixtures.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I have the same Starting 5 every month?

Yes. But you'll probably leave points on the table — the managers who adjust monthly will have an edge.

### What if I forget to set my lineup before the lock?

Your previous month's Starting 5 carries over. You won't be locked out, but you might be stuck with a suboptimal lineup. Don't rely on this.

### Can I see other managers' Starting 5?

Yes. On the League Home page, the "Starting Squads" section shows which teams each manager has started for the current month. This is public information.

### What if one of my teams gets relegated mid-season?

They remain on your roster and continue earning (or losing) points based on their matches in their domestic league.
